Michael Suen

Designer. Illustrator. Writer.
I make things for human beings

I'm a freelance designer, illustrator, and writer. My work emphasizes user-friendly experiences that blur work and play. In addition to running my own startup, Switchup, I collaborate with clients of all shapes and sizes to create websites, apps, and marketing campaigns that are both playful and effective.

Projects I've worked on have been featured on Wired, NPR, Kill Screen, and Voice of America. I've also presented on education and digital media culture at MIT, Bryn Mawr College, and Brown University. Formerly, I worked at the MIT spin-off  Learning Games Network, where I helped produce award-winning games and game-based learning initiatives.

Spawned in the tar pits of Los Angeles, raised by capitalist wolves in Hong Kong, and educated on the ice planet called Vermont, I now live in Cambridge, MA with my PlayStation 3.
